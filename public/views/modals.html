The song Puff the Magic Dragon troubled Percy deeply. What sort of boy would betray his best friend, and a dragon no less. Percy knew that poor Puff had no way to understand his rejection and Percy worried about the fatility of losing one's best friend. He remembers crying over it once or twice alone at night. Frosty the Snowman had a similar story, but Percy had first hand experience with snow people and knew they were just stacks of big snowballs made with wet sticky snow that had no love for anything. Frosty the Dragon is Percy's way of helping other children deal with Puff-like stories by substituting a totally worthless snow person for a beautiful and proud magical creature.


Childhood is confusing enough without songs about mythical features disappearing under emotional duress. When Percy was a young boy the song Puff the Magic Dragon troubled him deeply because he couldn't understand why any kid would be stupid enough to reject a freaking dragon, especially one that was such a great friend. It was inconceivable that a kid could be so foolishly disloyal to a Dragon. It probably had no way to understand why it was being rejected by it's best friend and Percy worried that Jackie Paper actually killed Puff. It was way too much for Percy to bear and he cried over it once or twice alone at night, but that was enough.

Frosty the Snowman was a similar sort of thing, but Percy had real first hand experience with snow people and knew they were just stacked big snowballs that could only be made when the snow was sticky wet. Making a snowman was usually a lot of fun unless adults wanted to enforce whatever rules and got mad when you threw a snowball, perfectly, into the bridge of their nose. Snowmen were pawn-like soldiers in the wars between siblings and neighborhood packs and never lasted very long. Percy instinctively understood that the whole song and story of Frosty was a fabrication by adults that didn't know what a snowman's purpose actually was.

In Frosty the Dragon, Percy was probably trying to exorcise his feelings for Puff by substituting the totally worthless Frosty for him. It does not bother Percy in the least that he has known several real snowmen and participated in their cruel demise while he has never seen a reptile larger than a crocodile, which was enough.
